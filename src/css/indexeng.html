Chas Academy’s current business model in the UX-field

My perception of Chas Academy's current business model in the UX-field contains some flaws. Here are some opportunities for improvement and where in the business model they have an impact.

The mix of UX design and requirements

The mix of UX design and requirements There were misplaced expectations regarding the distribution of UX design and requirements.

To remedy this, I suggest simplifying the distribution by clarifying the name of the education, for example “UX designer and Requirements Manager” and thus changing the “Jobs to be done” for the students.

Alternatively, review the curriculum and adjust the distribution and thereby change the value proposition.

Industry-specific

Despite the fact that Chas Academy is created by an industry company, this great potential is not fully exploited.

In the course, the students would have to solve real cases from Chas Visual Management to work with. It is important to get the student's experience and that the students can show a portfolio for job applications.

Another opportunity is to use the contact the school has with Chas Visual Management and the management team. For example, they can assist with cases and internships.

Chas Visual Management should use customers as potential channels for the education and promote the school on its websites, for example.

Practical training

Some students expected a more practical education than what it turned out to be.

To live up to the value proposition of practical training the school could build up an exercise-bank so that students always have access to exercises,

and collaborate with the developer class to experience what it's like to work in a team with developers, as well as frequent Figma lessons.

Unstructured

The last area for improvement is that students fail in their work because they experience an unstructured environment at school.



To fulfill the students “job to be done”, which means to learn UX, it’s important that the lessons are clear. They can solve the problem with having additional teachers by having them undergo an onboarding process to reduce misunderstandings and technical confusion.

One of the important things to learn about ux is to create a Ux portfolio that something have been misting.

After every large assignment they should have portofolio days to seek internship and work. In order to foster a stronger connection with the students, it is advisable to enhance feedback mechanisms and formulate a plan for working proactively rather than reactively, as is often perceived at present.

Additionally, investing more in staff/teachers could prove beneficial, ensuring resources are available to, for instance, verify the clarity of all assignments before their distribution to the class and to integrate more pedagogical elements into the learning experience.

In addition to these four areas, which we consider the most important, we have identified additional potential improvement measures.

These include utilizing social media for targeted marketing, exploring the possibility of offering a one-year program or individual courses to meet the needs of those seeking broader knowledge.

Encourage students who have completed or are currently undergoing the program to provide reviews on platforms such as Google, Reco, Trustpilot, or other review services.

The principal should regularly request student evaluations of the staff to create an environment where students feel comfortable providing honest feedback, recognizing the difficulty of addressing matters that involve the person making the inquiry.

Establish a standard for how students are expected to behave during lessons, such as camera usage, promoting questions and interaction, and communicate this at the beginning of the education program.

Summary

To summarize, the key improvement areas that we believe are crucial for Chas Academy to have more satisfied students and become a more attractive school are as follows:

1. Distribution of UX and requirements: Set clear expectations from the beginning to align with the industry standards.

2. Utilize the Chas Visual Management network: Become the most industry-specific school and leverage it as your unique selling proposition (USP).

3. Introduce more practical learning: Allow students to master the craft of UX design through hands-on experience, not solely relying on theoretical knowledge.



4. Enhance structure and planning: Invest more in staff to ensure that practical details do not detract from the overall learning experience.

Assignment on the Loopeli company page

I was tasked by Loopeli to improve the structure of their website and make it more tailored to their target audience. Our goal was to facilitate business users in quickly finding relevant information and thereby making decisions about integrating Loopeli into their operations. By understanding how businesses currently seek welfare aids for their operations and by analyzing user feedback and reflections on the app, I identified opportunities for improvements within Loopeli. These insights were then used to enhance the user experience and meet Loopeli's needs, with the aim of facilitating businesses to purchase and utilize Loopeli in their operations.

Research

First, I conducted an UX review of the website to identify interface issues and prepare for the interviews.

From the evaluation, several areas requiring improvement emerged. Users might find it challenging to navigate the website. There is an abundance of text that does not effectively serve its purpose.

I decided to utilize semi-structured interviews and user testing to explore users' thoughts, opinions, and behaviors, aiming to gain a deeper understanding of their needs.

This approach also provided a better foundation to confirm my hypotheses from the evaluation and identify additional areas requiring attention on the website.

Preparing for user tests and interviews

I formulated two questions to use as guidelines during the execution of user tests and interviews.

The purpose of these questions was to ensure a clear focus and avoid deviations from the central topic during both the tests and interviews.

Two questions

Answer 1: What type of information do users need to make decisions about integrating the product into their business, and is the information currently available to users? We are interested in observing how users navigate through various pages to search for information about Loopeli.

Answer 2: To enhance users' ability to find information on the website. Is it possible to facilitate users in making quicker decisions regarding the integration of the product into their business?

Following this, I began drafting emails to users to inquire about their participation in user tests.

Collection of insights

Before commencing the interviews and user tests, I introduced myself and explained the purpose of the study. I also informed participants about their rights, including adherence to WCAG guidelines and the guarantee of anonymity. Afterward, I requested permission to record the session. Then, I began with warm-up questions, followed by a think-aloud exercise regarding the menu. Users were then presented with various case scenarios for user testing. Finally, I concluded by asking participants if they had any specific thoughts after completing the tests.

I decided to apply the "think-aloud" method to the menu to observe users' expectations. It became evident that the menu names were ambiguously formulated and could be interpreted in various ways.

I conducted a test interview to assess if any changes were needed in the interview questions or user tests. The participant in the test interview struggled to immerse themselves in the case scenarios.

The question that needed to be changed was: "What type of information are you looking for when searching for aids for your business?" I revised the question to: "What characteristics should the products have to fit your business and your target audience? For purchasing them?" Afterwards, users were tasked with finding these characteristics on the website.

It was challenging for users to remember the characteristics, so they were written down on a piece of paper.

Den andra frågan i intervjuerna löd: "Vilken information behöver du för att fatta beslut om att införa produkterna till verksamheten?". Samma här skrev de ned frågan på ett papper.

The second question in the interviews was: "What information do you need to make decisions about integrating the products into your business?" Similarly, users also wrote this down on paper.



During the interviews, I realized the importance of the two questions above, as they clearly revealed what users are seeking when considering integrating products into their business while also helping me evaluate whether the necessary information was available on the website. These two tasks proved to be demanding for users and also turned out to be the most significant, resulting in a longer duration to complete them.

Suggestions for the structure

Both business users and private individuals had difficulty determining which page they were on and which page they had previously visited on the website.

Users experienced challenges in completing tasks due to an abundance of text that was overwhelming. Sometimes, information appeared unexpectedly for users.

There was also positive feedback about the website; its professional appearance was appreciated, as well as the use of inviting images of people.

Results

Based on the results from user tests and interviews, a new structural proposal has been developed and I subsequently received feedback from the project owner. Following this, a new version of the structure has been created for implementation on the website. I am developing various design proposals for the different pages of the website to enhance usability for the users. When creating tasks, I use user stories documented in Trello. This provides the product owner with a clear understanding of the purpose behind each task. In Trello, both the existing design and the new design are presented to facilitate understanding of the changes for developers and product owners. Following this, we conduct user tests on the website to evaluate whether users understand the new structure and design. Based on the feedback, further adjustments may be needed.

"Assignment on the Loopeli private pages"

I was hired by Loopeli to improve their webpage and to optimize the structure of the website and make it more adapted for their target audience.

To get a deep understanding of how they are interacting with the webpage,see how they find information about the app and get their opinions and thoughts.

To explore the user experience with connections to begin using loopeli, I strived to identify areas where improvements could be made.

Goals

1. Optimize the website layout and navigation to increase ease of use and create a unified experience.

2. Adapt the content and design on the webpage to better suit the target group and create a more relevant platform.

3. Conduct analysis to deepen the knowledge of user interaction with Loopeli's website and to identify specific challenges.

4. Evaluate users' perception of Loopeli as a company to clearly identify strengths, weaknesses and areas for improvement.

5. Study user navigation on Loopeli's websites to understand specific behavior patterns and to identify precise areas for navigation improvements.

6. Develop insights from user surveys to clearly define and prioritize specific areas where the sites can be improved.

7. Analyze how users initially interact with the website to identify concrete problems in the initial user flow.

8. Implement changes based on specific insights from user surveys to make it easier for users to acquire the Loopeli app on websites.

The purpose

"The purpose of user testing is to gain insight into how users interact/navigate through the websites and to truly identify usability issues, understand user needs, and improve the user experience through optimization of the website's structure and design.

We also assessed the overall impression of Loopeli as a company among users to strengthen the brand's connection and identify areas for improvement."

Research

I started by reviewing the website using Miro to identify potential issues and generate hypotheses about any shortcomings, such as interface and navigation challenges.

Additionally, I obtained an overview of the website's existing structure. Following that, I reached out to support to discuss the problems users have encountered.

Extra important user tests

The most important tasks during user testing.

Task 1: You've encountered Loopeli and are considering starting to use the app. Your task is to gather relevant information to facilitate your first use of the app.

The question is quite general and can be interpreted in various ways. What type of information do users seek and expect when they first start using the Loopeli app? Using the results showed that users encountered interface problems when starting to use Loopeli. All users ended up in the same situation and needed assistance to continue.

Before the actual user tests were conducted, I conducted a test interview to evaluate if any changes were needed.

I chose the think-aloud method for the menu as the first task because users couldn't rely on their own thoughts to get started, instead trying to relate to what they had seen on the website previously.

It was difficult to get hold of Loopeli's users to participate during the summer, so I had to use family members and coworkers who were in the same age group as the target audience of Loopeli users.

Collection of insights and proposed solutions

Collection of insights and proposed solutions After the interviews were transcribed into a document, they were compiled into a table in Miro.

Following the compilation in an impact map, the results were presented again to the product owners.

One of the most important insights from user tests is that the structure of the website needs improvement. Therefore, a new proposal for restructuring the website has been developed.

Another insight was that users were unsure which page of the website they were on, whether it was the company page or the private page. Therefore, we chose to redesign the menu with different colors to provide more clarity to users about which page they are on.

Additionally, an underline was added depending on which page the user is currently on.

Examensarbete

Bakgrund

Flertalet läkare och hälsoinstitut har uttryckt att barn under sju år bör använda digitala enheter högst 1 timme per dag. Av samlad erfarenhet vet vi att det är svårt att motivera barnen att slita sig från skärmen med risk för konflikter och irritation. Barn vill ha mer av det roliga.

Det finns även så oändligt mycket lärorikt på surfplattan och telefonen. Barnen kan hamna utanför sociala sammanhang om man tar bort skärmtiden från dem. Samtidigt finns det en ständig samhällsdebatt kring hur mycket ett barn ska sitta framför skärmar.

Jag ville göra ett projekt från start eftersom jag vill bli mer säker på “the design thinking process". Idén kom från en vän som berättade vilka problem hon hade att få hennes barn att slita sig från skärmen.

Syftet

Att förstå hur föräldrar hanterar bråk i vardagen och hur de undviker bråk med barn.

Jag ska bli mer utmanad av projektet och lära mig mer om "The Design Thinking Process". Få erfarenhet av att intervjua andra personer, få en djupare förståelse för vad man bör tänka på som UX-designer.

Vad gör barn när de sitter framför digitala TOOL? Förstå barnens behov av att sitta framför skärmen.

Målet

Att föräldrar ska slippa bråka om skärmtiden. Att få en djupare förståelse för“The Design Thinking Process" för att på så sätt bli en bättre UX-designer.

Jag ska få en bättre förståelse över vad jag bör tänka på när jag intervjuar användare om vad som kan förbättras. Få fram helt nya insikter från datainsamlingen om målgrupperna för att på så sätt förstå deras behov och mål, för att i slutändan kunna möta målgruppens behov med en klickbar prototyp.

Barnet är i behov av att få veta vad som ska göras efter att speltiden är slut och vill höra orsaken.
Barnet är i behov av att bli förvarnad att speltiden håller på ta slut.
Barnet har behov av att få vara med och bestämma vad hen ska göra efter speltiden.
Barnet har behov av fasta regler i vardagen.När barnet har stort behovan att hålla sig aktiv och stimulerad glömmer hen bort iPad eller liknande när hen gör andra aktiviteter.

Föräldars beteendertype

Alla föräldrar har gemensamt tre karaktäristik/beteenden; de hittar på mer aktiviteter och försöker avleda med aktiviteter, egentid och undvika bråk.

Freestyle-föräldern säger till barnet att avsluta skärmtiden på direkten. Freestyle-föräldern begränsar inte aktivt skärmtiden och förbereder inte barnet.

Freestyle-föräldern är mer inställd på att det blir bråk när barnet ska avsluta skärmtiden.

Planerar-föräldern begränsar aktivt skärmtiden genom att använda ett hjälpmedel för att hålla tiden(app eller alarmklocka) som även förvarnar tiden snart slut.

De har fasta regler för när barnet får använda iPad/TV/dator och det förklarar reglerna och varför de måste avsluta skärmtiden

Rutin-föräldern begränsar inte aktivt skärmtiden, istället har barnet rutiner i vardagen som är slags olika delmål; exempelvis att titta ner tills maten är klar eller eller borsta tänderna, efter dessa rutiner får barnet sitta vid sin iPad.

Rutin-föräldern förvarnar barnet när skärmtiden snart är slut och få titta klart beroende på hur långt det är kvar av programmet eller spelet.

Barnets beteendertype

Det som skiljer barnets beteendettyp åt är hur stort intresset är för iPad/TV/dator. Beror på hur många andra aktiviteter som gör att de hinner eller inte hinner titta på iPaden.

När barnet gör andra aktiviteter glömmer barnet bort iPaden. Ett annat sätt de skiljer sig åt är hur mycket barnet själv får bestämma.

Programideér

Från datainsamlingen kom jag fram till två olika programidéer.

Den första idén var att barnet skulle välja en aktivitet för att sen starta skärmtiden.

Den andra idén var att barnet varje vecka fick hämta ut 4st spelbiljetter på 30 minuter styck. Under veckan fick barnet använda biljetter för att få tid att spela.

Föräldrarna fick bestämma när biljetterna skulle användas. Jag beslutade mig för att slå ihop de båda idéerna.

Flödesdiagram

Valet att använda flödesdiagram kom till för att jag var osäker på vilka vyer som behövdes i prototypen.

Det blev mer tydligt för mig vilka vyer som skulle behövas när jag gjorde skisserna och tog fram två olika förslag på flödesdiagram.

Lite osäker dock på vilken som är bäst. Slutflödet blev att barnet skulle hämta biljetter och välja en aktivitet för att sedan starta skärmtiden.

Program-research

Jag använde mig av färgen rött i gränssnittet därför att färgen rött har en förmåga att dra till sig ögat och användare klickar gärna på röda färgen.

Jag har läst olika artiklar om hur färger påverkar människors beteende. Jag valde även könsneutrala färger några gånger till hi-fi-prototypen eftersom det skulle passa båda könen.

Användar-testerna

Jag började skissa på papper och göra en low-fi-prototyp över vyerna. Gick över till att göra hi-fi-prototyper över hur barn-användare reagerade på färger,

ville även se hur barnen reagerade på rörelse i spelet. Det kan vara svårt för barn att föreställa sig ett program/app på penna och papper när barnet utför användartester.

Jag beslutade mig för att göra användartester för att se om användare förstod flödet och gränssnittet, under testerna kunde jag göra justeringar på prototypen samtidigt.

På barnens tester upptäckte jag direkt att de aldrig använt en dator tidigare. Detta blev ett stort problem.

Barnen tyckte det blev för svårt och läskigt att testa något de aldrig har gjort tidigare, och dessutom inför en okänd person.

Om det inte hade varit för att tekniken skulle strula så skulle jag ha utfört fler användartester på barn. Det genomfördes 5 användartester på barn som var mellan 3-9 år gamla och 8 användartester på vuxna. Det blev fler användartester på föräldrar eftersom de kunde hantera en dator.

Resultat från användar-testerna

Här är några resultat från användartesterna, att test var att användare tryckte på en karusell för att välja aktiviteter. Tidigare kunde man bara dra i spaken.

Jag fick en kommentar från användare om att animationen tog för lång tid för att hämta biljetter. Därför ändrade jag till snabbare hastighet på animationen.

En observation var att barnens ögon drogs till allt rött som blinkade, speciellt de yngre barnen 3-4 år. Flera användare tvekade på hur de skulle hantera meny.

Jag hade gjort om menyn men problemen blev inte helt löst och det kommer behövas göra fler användar-tester för att lösa det.

Slutresultat

Jag har åstadkommit det jag har planerat att göra och känner mig rätt nöjd med resultatet och med det arbete jag har lagt ned.

Just nu finns det hi-fi-prototyper bara i desktopläge och inte i mobilläge, på grund av att det inte fanns tillräckligt med tid för att göra mer.

Något som saknades i appen var ljud. Det var tänkt att bävern och flodhästen skulle bland annat välkomna och motivera barnet.

Därför använde jag mig av texter och pratbubblor.

Jag är inte jättenöjd med menyn därför att användare hade svårt att navigera i den. Nästa steg är att förbättra menyn och designa de 4 sista vyerna på slutet.

Jag har arbetat med det här projektet i olika perioder under en längre tid. Jag borde ha tagit ett mindre projekt för det blev väldigt stort att arbeta helt själv med.









Soup kitchens Uppsala



The goal of the soup kitchens were to create a passing place for people who would’ve never met otherwise and to remove prejudices.

The soup kitchens were a place to discuss the situation for the homeless of Uppsala. The goal of the soup kitchens were to highlight the problem of homelessness.

In the beginning we were six people planning everything together and we had clothing collections and clothing storage, transporting things away and also had contact with the media.

My responsibilities were to have contact with supermarkets, tent rental and with private individuals who lent us equipment. I designed the soup kitchens logotype, several posters and clothing collections and organized transport by car and flatbed.

During the soup kitchen clothes and food were distributed to people who needed it. Many of the people were staying and talking.

Thanks to social media we got some big attention and commitment was created and many private individuals came to leave clothes, fruit, food, and some people even got haircuts.

What I remember most from my first soup kitchens was that we felt so much joy from all the people who came and the soup kitchens were very successful.

After the first soup kitchen we got more volunteers which made everything more interesting to plan.

The soup kitchens have taught me a lot about planning events and convincing companies to sponsor us. After having been active in soup kitchen Uppsala for a year, I felt it was time for others to take over.

Soup kitchens in all the cities it was active in are now discontinued and soup kitchen Uppsala lasted between the years 2013-2015.
